Salcedo

History

Salcedo is a municipality located in the Hermanas Mirabal Province of the Dominican Republic, and it has a rich and fascinating history. The town played an important role in the struggle for independence in the 19th century, with Gregorio Luperón being one of its most notable figures. Salcedo continued to grow and develop in the 20th century, and it is now home to several important institutions, including the University of Technology and Applied Sciences and the Centro de Estudios del Cibao.

Salcedo is also known for its association with the Mirabal sisters, who were political activists and national heroes. Three of the sisters were brutally murdered by the dictatorship of Rafael Trujillo in 1960, and their deaths are commemorated every year on November 25th. Today, Salcedo is a vibrant and thriving community, attracting visitors from all over the world who come to experience its unique culture and natural beauty. Whether you're interested in history, nature, or simply soaking up the local atmosphere, Salcedo is a must-see destination in the Dominican Republic.

5 point of interest in Salcedo

  1. Museo Hermanas Mirabal: This museum is dedicated to the legacy of the Mirabal sisters, who were political activists and national heroes. The museum features exhibits about their lives and the struggle for democracy in the Dominican Republic.
  2. Parque Nacional Salto del Límon: This national park is located just outside of Salcedo and features a stunning waterfall, as well as opportunities for hiking and wildlife viewing.
  3. Casa Museo Gregorio Luperón: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of Gregorio Luperón, who was a military leader and national hero. The museum features exhibits about his life and the struggle for independence in the Dominican Republic.
  4. La Plazoleta: This is a central plaza in the heart of Salcedo, which features a beautiful fountain and is a popular gathering place for locals.
  5. Carnaval de Salcedo: This is an annual festival that takes place in February and is one of the largest and most vibrant carnivals in the Dominican Republic. The festival features music, dance, and colorful costumes, and attracts visitors from all over the world.

My favorite food from Salcedo

My favorite food from Salcedo is Dulce de batata con coco. This is a dessert made with sweet potatoes, coconut milk, and sugar. It has a creamy, sweet flavor and is a popular treat in Salcedo and throughout the Dominican Republic.
